What it does

  • Reports when done. A short summary lands on your phone — what happened, what needs deciding.
  • Takes the reply back. Answer in plain English; it arrives as if you'd typed it at the desk.
  • Shows your limits. Tap /usage and your subscription window comes to the chat, not the browser.
  • Speaks and listens. Send a voice note, ask for a voice answer. Both run on your machine.

What it won't do

  • Slash commands, except /usage. Over this bridge they arrive as plain text, and nothing runs them.
  • Switch models mid-session. Set it at launch — abs --model opus. Asking nicely doesn't help.
  • Interrupt a running task. Messages are read between turns, not during one.
  • Sandbox anything. Whoever can message the bot can drive a session on your machine.
